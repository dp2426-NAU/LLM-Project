Final Synthesized Answer
The attention mechanism in transformer models enables dynamic, context-aware information retrieval by computing weighted relationships between all positions in a sequence simultaneously. Unlike recurrent architectures, transformers apply scaled dot-product attention: Attention(Q,K,V) = softmax(QKᵀ / √d_k) × V, where Q, K, V are learned linear projections of the input. The √d_k scaling prevents vanishing gradients in high-dimensional spaces. Multi-head attention runs h parallel heads, each learning to attend to different semantic subspaces, concatenating results back to model dimension — enabling simultaneous capture of syntax, coreference, and semantics.

For fine-tuning, LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation) freezes all pretrained weights and injects two trainable matrices A and B into attention layers: W′ = W + BA, where rank r (4–16) « d (4096), reducing trainable parameters by up to 10,000× while staying within 1–2% of full fine-tuning performance. QLoRA adds 4-bit NormalFloat quantization, enabling 65B-parameter model fine-tuning on a single 48GB GPU with <1% benchmark degradation on MMLU and HumanEval.
