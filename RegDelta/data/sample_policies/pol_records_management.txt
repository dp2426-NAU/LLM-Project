Policy: Electronic Records Management
Policy ID: POL-REC-001
Department: Compliance & Operations
Version: 3.1
Last Reviewed: 2023-09-15

1. Purpose and Scope
This policy governs the creation, retention, storage, and destruction of electronic records
generated by all employees of the firm. It applies to all business units and geographic locations.

2. Record Retention Schedule
2.1 Electronic Communications
All electronic communications including email, Bloomberg messages, and approved instant
messaging platforms must be retained for a minimum of 3 years from the date of creation.

2.2 Trade Records
Order records, confirmations, and related trading communications must be retained for 6 years.

2.3 Client Records
Account opening documentation and client correspondence must be retained for 7 years
following account closure.

3. Storage Requirements
3.1 All records must be stored on firm-approved WORM-compliant storage systems.
3.2 Personal email accounts and non-approved messaging platforms are prohibited for business use.
3.3 Monthly integrity checks must be performed and documented by the IT Operations team.

4. Third-Party Storage
The firm may use approved cloud storage providers that have been vetted by Information Security
and confirmed to meet WORM compliance requirements. All third-party providers must sign a
Data Processing Agreement (DPA) prior to use.

5. Supervisory Review
5.1 Compliance must review a random sample of electronic communications monthly.
5.2 Written Supervisory Procedures (WSPs) are reviewed and updated annually.
5.3 Any exceptions to this policy require written approval from the Chief Compliance Officer.

6. Violations
Employees who use non-approved platforms for business communications or fail to preserve
records as required will be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.

7. Regulatory Examination
In the event of a regulatory examination, the firm will respond to requests for records
within 24 hours unless an extension is granted by the examiner.
