Policy: Capital Adequacy and Stress Testing
Policy ID: POL-RISK-002
Department: Risk Management
Version: 2.3
Last Reviewed: 2023-11-30

1. Purpose
This policy defines the firm's approach to maintaining adequate capital levels and conducting
stress tests in line with regulatory requirements and internal risk appetite.

2. Capital Requirements
2.1 The firm must maintain net capital in excess of the greater of:
    - $250,000 minimum net capital requirement
    - 2% of aggregate debit items (broker-dealer standard method)

2.2 Early Warning Notification
When net capital falls below 120% of the required minimum, the firm must immediately
notify the SEC and FINRA and prepare a capital restoration plan.

3. Stress Testing Framework
3.1 The firm conducts stress tests on a quarterly basis covering:
    - Market risk scenarios (equity crash, rate spike, credit spread widening)
    - Liquidity stress (3-day, 30-day, and 90-day outflow scenarios)
    - Operational stress (key-person loss, system failure)

3.2 Results are reviewed by the Risk Committee and presented to the Board quarterly.

3.3 Regulatory stress test results (where applicable) are submitted to the Federal Reserve
    and/or SEC within prescribed deadlines.

4. Liquidity Management
4.1 The firm maintains a Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) buffer of at least 110%.
4.2 A Contingency Funding Plan (CFP) is maintained and tested annually.

5. Reporting
5.1 FOCUS Reports must be filed monthly with FINRA.
5.2 Annual audited financial statements must be filed within 60 days of fiscal year end.
5.3 All capital and liquidity metrics are reported to senior management weekly.

6. Documentation Retention
All stress test models, assumptions, results, and management review documentation
must be retained for 7 years.
