SEC Exchange Act Rule 17a-4 — Electronic Records Retention
Version: 2023 Final Rule
Effective Date: January 3, 2023
Regulatory Body: SEC

Section 1.1 Scope and Applicability
This rule applies to all broker-dealers registered under Section 15 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934.
The requirements herein govern the preservation, accessibility, and security of electronic records
generated in the ordinary course of business.

Section 1.2 Retention Periods
(a) Electronic communications, including email, instant messages, and chat communications related to
the broker-dealer's business as such, must be retained for a period of not less than 3 years.
The first 2 years must be in an easily accessible place.

(b) Records of original entry must be retained for not less than 3 years.

(c) All other records must be retained for not less than 6 years.

Section 1.3 Storage Format Requirements
All records required to be maintained under this rule shall be stored in a non-rewriteable,
non-erasable format known as WORM (Write Once, Read Many).

(a) Electronic storage media must preserve the records exclusively in a non-rewriteable,
non-erasable format.

(b) The broker-dealer must verify automatically the quality and accuracy of the storage
media recording process.

Section 1.4 Third-Party Arrangements
A broker-dealer may employ a third-party storage provider, provided the provider meets the
technical requirements of this section and the broker-dealer obtains a written undertaking from
the provider that it will make available to securities regulators, upon request, any records
stored on its systems.

Section 2.1 Supervisory Procedures
Every broker-dealer shall establish written supervisory procedures (WSPs) reasonably designed
to ensure compliance with applicable federal securities laws and FINRA rules.

(a) WSPs must be reviewed and updated at least annually.

(b) A designated principal must approve all WSPs prior to implementation.

Section 2.2 Audit Trail Requirements
Broker-dealers must maintain a complete audit trail of all electronic communications
that can demonstrate the integrity and authenticity of records.

Section 3.1 Examination Access
The broker-dealer must furnish promptly to any representative of the Commission or self-regulatory
organization copies of any required record. Access must be provided within 24 hours of request
during an examination.
