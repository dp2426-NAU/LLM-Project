SEC Exchange Act Rule 17a-4 — Electronic Records Retention
Version: 2024 Amended Rule
Effective Date: June 1, 2024
Regulatory Body: SEC

Section 1.1 Scope and Applicability
This rule applies to all broker-dealers registered under Section 15 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934,
and has been extended to include investment advisers registered under the Investment Advisers Act of 1940
with assets under management exceeding $1 billion.
The requirements herein govern the preservation, accessibility, and security of electronic records
generated in the ordinary course of business, including records on off-channel communications platforms.

Section 1.2 Retention Periods
(a) Electronic communications, including email, instant messages, chat communications, and communications
on third-party platforms (including but not limited to WhatsApp, Signal, and other messaging applications)
related to the broker-dealer's business must be retained for a period of not less than 7 years.
The first 2 years must be in an easily accessible place.

(b) Records of original entry must be retained for not less than 7 years.

(c) All other records must be retained for not less than 7 years.

Section 1.3 Storage Format Requirements
All records required to be maintained under this rule shall be stored in a non-rewriteable,
non-erasable format known as WORM (Write Once, Read Many).

(a) Electronic storage media must preserve the records exclusively in a non-rewriteable,
non-erasable format. Cloud-based storage is now explicitly permitted provided it meets WORM compliance
standards verified by an independent third-party auditor annually.

(b) The broker-dealer must verify automatically the quality and accuracy of the storage
media recording process. Verification logs must themselves be retained.

(c) NEW: Off-channel communications — firms must implement technical controls to capture and
archive communications on all platforms used for business communications, whether firm-provided
or personal devices used for business purposes.

Section 1.4 Third-Party Arrangements
A broker-dealer may employ a third-party storage provider, provided the provider meets the
technical requirements of this section and the broker-dealer obtains a written undertaking from
the provider that it will make available to securities regulators, upon request, any records
stored on its systems within 4 hours (previously 24 hours).

Section 2.1 Supervisory Procedures
Every broker-dealer shall establish written supervisory procedures (WSPs) reasonably designed
to ensure compliance with applicable federal securities laws and FINRA rules.

(a) WSPs must be reviewed and updated at least semi-annually (previously annually).

(b) A designated principal must approve all WSPs prior to implementation.

(c) NEW: WSPs must specifically address the capture, archival, and supervision of
off-channel communications including personal device usage policies.

Section 2.2 Audit Trail Requirements
Broker-dealers must maintain a complete audit trail of all electronic communications
that can demonstrate the integrity and authenticity of records. Audit trails must now
include metadata capturing the originating device type and platform for each communication.

Section 3.1 Examination Access
The broker-dealer must furnish promptly to any representative of the Commission or self-regulatory
organization copies of any required record. Access must be provided within 4 hours of request
during an examination (reduced from 24 hours).

Section 3.2 Self-Reporting Obligations (NEW)
Firms that discover a failure to capture or retain records as required by this rule must
self-report to the Commission within 30 calendar days of discovery. Self-reporting may be
considered a mitigating factor in any resulting enforcement action.

Section 4.1 Penalties
Violations of this rule may result in fines up to $10 million per violation (increased from $5 million),
disgorgement of profits, and suspension or revocation of broker-dealer registration.
